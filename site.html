You're reading about the website which ultimately got me arrested by the FBI. Read about my charges here or read more about my prison experience here or go back to the root.

Why did you do it?

It started as a proof of concept. My beloved BallStreams---a website to watch NBA games---pulled the rug and NBA fans like myself were left SOL. I made a proof of concept, posted it on Reddit, and eventually I put a paywall on it to see if anyone would bite. I didn't think I'd ever reach product market fit. Oops, I did. While it was, indeed, a subscription website, the motivation for starting and maintaining the website weren't financial---I'd argue it was the opposite, even before being charged by the US Government.

How did it work?

A lot of engineering. For nerds: It was a Rails-based application with 227 models, backed by a 1.2TB database---hardly your average streaming website. It was a monolith with two microservices for authenticating into a third-party service. The API for the apps was built with Grape and sat atop the monolith. I developed Caffeinate to handle customer lifecycle emails, and later released it as an open source package where it remains the most popular package of its kind for Rails applications. Many of the patterns used in the application are discussed in my book Caboose on the Loose.
