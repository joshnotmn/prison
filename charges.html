What were your charges?

One count of fraud by accessing a protected computer and exceeds authorized access, one count of wire fraud, one count of extortion, one count of interstate threats, and one count of illicit digital transmission.

It is important to note I was never indicted by a grand jury, only charged by complaint. A jury would have had a difficult time getting the extortion and interstate threats charge to stick.

I ultimately pleaded guilty to the unauthorized computer access charge.

Why the unauthorized computer access charge?

The content I was providing on my website was retrieved with cURL commands using -H 'Cookies: #{cookies}'.

Why the wire fraud charge?

Any fraud committed over a government-owned wire is wire fraud. I used the internet.

Why the extortion?

I reported a series of important bugs to Major League Baseball around the time that an investigation opened into my website after I ran my mouth about the sports leagues in a popular online news outlet. Anyway, they dangled a carrot in front of me and asked how much I valued the bugs at and how I'd get there, so I linked to Shopify's bug bounty calculator.

Bam, you just tried to extort Major League Baseball for $150,000, and we're going to run with this.

Wait, what were the bugs?

I won't say. There were eight of them and they could not have been more unrelated to streaming. Any other organization would have done backflips over server racks.

Why the interstate threats?

When I originally found the bugs I went to a known baseball journalist and suggested I had some really important information on how baseball is treating its fans and their security.

After they patched the bugs, I wanted to publish my findingsâ€”you know, for internet points. They got word that I had talked to this journalist and threw a fit.

What were you looking at for time?

News said a lot of years but of course you plead guilty to lesser charges.

Going into sentencing I was looking at a little more than seven years. I got 36 months. My sentencing guidelines were so high because of the loss amount.

Did you really make $3 million dollars?

No. Major League Baseball assumed I had 11,000 customers because I wrote a guest post for a website that doesn't exist anymore talking about how I scaled someone's business to 11,000 customers.

Less than that.

I'm really proud of what I made, yes. I come from a broken home, and the community was a home to me for six years, so I have solace there.
