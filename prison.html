How long were you in prison?

A little more than 18 months: January of 2024 to August of 2025.

Where were you?

I was housed at FCI Thomson in Thomson, Illinois. You may have heard about AUSP Thomson on the news, NPR, or The Marshall Project; its claim to fame was killing inmates who were locked in their cells 23 hours a day. Weird. Anyway, they changed the custody classification while keeping the same staff. It didn't go well, granted nobody has died yet, an officer did get caught doing some nasty-nast with inmates.

Why weren't you in Minnesota?

The BOP had just opened this prison when I was designated. I should have been at camp but they had bed space issues, so they put me here in the interim and they never allowed me to leave.

What was it like?

Boring as hell and not at all like you see on TV, I promise you.

What did you do all day?

I walked the track a lot, played a bit of basketball, and watched too much TV. There was really nothing better to do.

Did you meet cool people?

Depends on your definition cool, but probably not.

What did you learn?

About how broken the system is, or about myself?

Yourself, sure.

I am more social than I ever thought I could be.

And about the system?

The system incarcerates people for years and expects them to go back into society and do something with their life. The truth is that the programming that is offered is a world-class joke and 99% of inmates don't really do all that well when they're out.
